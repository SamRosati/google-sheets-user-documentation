# Troubleshooting Guide

Even for experienced users, spreadsheets can occasionally behave unexpectedly. If you run into trouble, check the solutions below.

| **If you see/experience...** | **It likely means...** | **Try this solution...** |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **A red triangle in the cell corner** | There is a formula error. | Hover your mouse over the cell to read the specific error message (e.g., a typo in your formula). |
| **The formula displays as text (e.g., you see "=SUM...")** | The cell is formatted as "Plain Text" or you forgot the equals sign. | 1. Ensure the formula starts with `=`. 2. Go to **Format** > **Number** and select **Automatic**. |
| **#REF! Error** | A cell referenced in your formula was deleted. | Press `[Ctrl]` + `[Z]` to undo the deletion, or click the cell to update the range manually. |
| **"Alternating colors" is greyed out** | You haven't selected a range of cells. | Click and drag your mouse to highlight a group of cells first, then try the **Format** menu again. |
| **A "Loading..." message that won't go away** | Your internet connection is unstable. | Refresh your browser page. Because Google Sheets is cloud-based, it saves every few seconds, so you won't lose your work. |
